A **lens** is a standing view a Mind authors itself. One agent turn composes a
canvas board and publishes it, and it renders as a live panel on the Chamber
surface with no hand-coded UI. The view is produced by an agent, not written by
hand. It is a distinctive durable output of Chamber: the reasoning does not just
leave a transcript, it leaves a live board you can keep open, and keep updating
under the same subject id.

Its sibling is the **exhibit**: a point-in-time deliverable a room discussion
tabled (an assessment, a plan, a findings board). The two share one pipeline and
one store but answer different questions. See
[Exhibits: the deliverable sibling](#exhibits-the-deliverable-sibling) below.

## A lens is the output of a turn

A lens is not a participant and not an agent. It is the product of a single Mind's
turn, the same way genesis produces a Mind and a room turn produces a reply. Because
it is a solo turn with no peer, a lens is orthogonal to rooms: it does not ride the
room driver and does not depend on a strategy. (Minds talking to each other is a
[room](../rooms/); a Mind authoring a view is a turn.)

## Lens, transcript, and briefing

A lens is one of several boards on the Chamber surface. It helps to know how it
differs from the others by author and update model:

| Artifact | Producer | Purpose | Update model |
|---|---|---|---|
| Room board | The room driver | Show the attributed conversation | Updated after each committed turn |
| Lens | A Mind or the lens workflow | Preserve an interpretation you keep watching | Re-authored explicitly under a stable id |
| Exhibit | A room turn via `chamber_table_exhibit` | Preserve a deliverable a discussion produced | Tabled once; deleted when no longer needed, or with its room |
| Briefing | The rib's attention gate plus one agent turn | Surface newly ended rooms or changed lenses | Runs only when its watermark finds new substance |
| Digest | Watermark-gated agent turn | Standing synthesis of current Chamber state | Runs only when fingerprint detects a change since last digest |
| Roster and index boards | Deterministic collectors | Show persistent Chamber state | Cadence or explicit refresh |

## The board, and the pipeline it rides

A board is a generic canvas payload: a title, an optional status, and a few kinds
of section (stats, rows, cards, segments, and so on). It is the harness's own
shape, not Chamber's, and the harness already knows how to render one. See the
keelson docs for the board contract.

Authoring a lens uses the same pipeline as everything else in Chamber. The turn
emits a board, the rib validates it fail-closed before anything renders, and the
bound view draws it live. A board that does not validate is rejected loudly rather
than rendered half-formed or dropped without a word.

:::caution[Validation protects the canvas contract, not the conclusion]
A valid lens has the correct board shape. Its claims are still agent-authored
judgments. Weigh them against the room transcript and the lens's provenance, not the
fact that it rendered cleanly.
:::

## A panel per subject

Each lens is keyed by a short subject id, and each new subject gets its own panel
on the Chamber surface. Re-authoring the same subject updates that panel in place
rather than adding another. By default a lens changes only when a Mind or workflow
authors the same id again. One exception: if a lens was authored with a `lens-note`
action attached, an operator can append a note to it directly from its panel without
re-authoring the full board. The rib sets no fixed limit on how many lenses can
exist; the harness enforces a per-surface ceiling.

Every lens panel also carries its own verb in the head's **⋯** menu (**Retire
lens…**), so a panel can be put away without hunting its index card. It confirms
before acting. An exhibit has no panel: its verbs ride the card in its room's Tabled
section.

## A living lens re-composes itself

Authoring a lens with `refresh` set turns the standing view into a **living** one:

```
chamber_emit_lens { id: "morning-brief", board: {...}, refresh: {} }
```

`refresh` names a workflow (default: the bundled **chamber-lens-refresh**
re-author) and an optional `cadenceMs` (default: one hour; floored at 30s). The
lens's panel then re-runs that workflow with input `lens` = the lens id: on its
cadence while the surface is open, and on demand from the **Refresh** verb its
index card gains. The workflow re-reads the record and re-emits a board under the
same id. If that board actually changed, the panel's "updated Xm ago" clock and the
index card's freshness advance with it; if the refresh found nothing to change, the
re-emit is a no-op and the clock holds. So the clock tracks the board, not the
turn: a lens whose content is a measurement must never read as current merely
because a refresh ran. The cost of that is a board which was re-measured and did
not move reads as old, which is the safer of the two available errors.

Two rules keep the backing honest. Re-authoring the lens **without** `refresh`
preserves the existing backing (the refresh turn itself re-emits the board and
must not strip its own wiring), and a `refresh` object PATCHES it: an omitted
field keeps its prior value, so a cadence-only re-author cannot swap a bespoke
workflow for the default. Only `refresh: null` removes the backing.
And a refresh through the bundled re-author is a paid agent turn, so the cadence
defaults lean quiet: the panel refreshes while you're watching, not overnight (the
server heartbeat deliberately skips per-lens regions). A workflow of your own costs
whatever it is made of, which need not be a turn at all.

## A data lens names a workflow of its own

The bundled lens authors can read files — `Read`, `Glob`, `Grep`, and nothing else.
A lens over a repo, a report, or a facts file is grounded in it: the turn goes and
reads the thing rather than composing from the subject string. What it cannot do is
run a command or reach anything live, and it is asked to say so plainly rather than
invent numbers.

So `chamber-lens-refresh` can re-read a file, and otherwise re-composes from the
board it already wrote. That is enough for a lens that interprets a subject or
watches a file, and not enough for one that *measures* something live: to re-derive
those numbers, a lens needs a refresh workflow that goes and gets them. The floor
stays there deliberately — loosening the bundled path far enough to reach live data
would hand a shell to every lens turn, and that privilege belongs in a workflow file
a person wrote and placed themselves.

Which raises the question of what a lens is allowed to name. `refresh.workflow` is
agent-authored data, so if a lens could name any workflow on the machine, a Mind
could point one at arbitrary code and have the panel run it on a cadence. The
harness closes that off: it runs a panel's refresh **only for a workflow a rib
contributed**, and refuses anything from the general catalog. A workflow file in
the global workflows dir can never qualify, however correct it is.

So Chamber contributes them for you. A workflow file placed in its
`lens-workflows/` directory is contributed as `chamber-lens-<filename>`, which a
lens may then name. The filename decides the name, overriding whatever the file's
own `name:` field says. Chamber vouches for these files, and the trust boundary is
that *you* put them there: a workflow's `bash` nodes run as written, so the
directory deserves the same care as anything else you would run yourself.
Contributions are collected once at startup, so a newly added file needs a
`keelson stop && keelson start` before its cadence works. Naming a workflow Chamber
does not contribute earns a caveat in the emit's reply, which is the only place you
would hear about it: the refresh itself is refused silently.

`refresh.inputs` carries that workflow's own parameters, so a lens id can be chosen
for what the subject *is* rather than for what its producer needs to parse back out
of it. They reach the workflow as `KEELSON_INPUTS_<key>`, alongside `lens` (the lens
id), which always wins: it is the one input the refresh contract guarantees, so a
stored input of that name cannot take it over.

Both species take a backing on these same terms, with one asymmetry: an HTML lens's
`refresh` **must** name its `workflow`. There is no default for a page, because
nothing generic could re-compose one. The bundled canvas re-author works by reading
the prior board back through `chamber_list_lenses`, and a designed page is far past
what a tool result can carry; a page re-authored from its subject alone would instead
redesign itself on every tick, never landing on identical markup, so its clock would
read "just now" forever and each tick would buy a turn for a page that says what it
said before. A living HTML lens is one its own producer re-derives.

## Provenance the card carries

A lens can record who authored it and why, so the Lenses index reads as a living
library rather than an anonymous pile. The authoring turn may attach three optional
fields: a **scope** (the board's kind, like "status board" or "checklist"), a
**reason** (a short note on what the latest authoring changed), and a
**maintainingMind** (the authoring Mind's own name). The index card shows
each one when present, alongside an "updated" time the server stamps whenever the
board changes. None of it is required.

`scope` and `maintainingMind` are durable identity, so they follow the same rule as
`refresh`: re-authoring without them preserves the existing values, and `null` clears
one. That matters most for a custom `refresh.workflow`, which has no reason to know
it would otherwise have to re-state a maintainer it never set. `reason` is the
exception: it describes one authoring, so omitting it clears it.

## Ways to author one

- **As a workflow.** Run `/lens {subject}`, or
  `keelson workflow run chamber-lens --arguments "{subject}"`. One turn composes a board for
  the subject and publishes it. This is the standalone entry point.
- **Not during a room.** A room turn's publish seam is the exhibit tool, not the
  lens tool: a findings summary after a discussion is a deliverable, so it lands in
  that room's own board (below). Authoring a standing lens stays a deliberate,
  named act: the workflow or a direct chat tool call.
- **As an HTML canvas.** A Mind (or any tool call) can invoke `chamber_emit_lens_html`
  with a literal HTML string, rendered in a sandboxed iframe on the Chamber surface
  under the Lenses group. With an `id` it is per-subject and persisted like a board
  lens, and it takes the same `refresh` backing, so a designed page can be living too.
  Without an `id` it lands on the legacy fixed key `rib:chamber:lens-html`, in-memory
  only, and re-publishing replaces that single shared panel; having no record, it
  takes no backing. An HTML lens has no index card, so its panel-head
  **⋯ → Retire lens…** is its one *surface* delete path (the sandboxed iframe rightly
  can't reach destructive verbs).

The two species are separate lenses in separate stores, sharing nothing but the id
string. So one id can name both a board and a page, which is the natural pairing when
a subject wants a structured view and a designed report. `chamber_list_lenses` returns
both, marking each row's `kind` so the two are told apart, and `chamber_retire_lens`
takes an optional `kind` for the same reason: an id naming both must say which, since
a retire cannot be undone.

## Exhibits: the deliverable sibling

An **exhibit** is what a room leaves on the table: the assessment, plan, or
findings board a discussion converged on. Where a lens is a standing view with a
maintainer and a freshness clock, an exhibit is its moment: it does not go
stale, and a new discussion tables a *new* exhibit rather than updating an old
one. The filing test: would re-running it produce the same thing? A lens says
yes (same subject, fresher content); an exhibit says no.

Mechanically the two share everything but where they are read. An exhibit rides the
same store, the same per-id key family, and the same fail-closed publish pipeline;
its record carries `kind: "exhibit"`, and it registers a key but no panel of its own.
Its card, in the Tabled section of its room's board, offers **Open** and a confirmed
**Delete** where a lens panel offers Retire.

Provenance is the load-bearing difference. An exhibit's card names the producing
room, and that field is **witnessed, not claimed**: the room driver stamps
`sourceRoom` after seeing `chamber_table_exhibit` fire in a turn it ran. The tool
itself has no `sourceRoom` input, so a Mind cannot attribute an exhibit to a room
it wasn't in. That stamp is also the way in: it is what puts the exhibit in its
room's **Tabled** section, and what a room delete follows to take its exhibits with
it: a stamped exhibit is reachable on the surface exactly as long as its room is. An
exhibit the driver never witnessed carries no stamp, so it reaches no room board and
no cascade collects it; `chamber_list_exhibits` and `chamber_delete_exhibit` stay the
way to find and remove one. A Mind that declares the `lens` capability holds the
exhibit tool in rooms.

## Every panel uses the same canvas contract

Every panel on the Chamber surface is a published board, never hand-coded UI, but they
do not all share an author. A lens, the briefing, and the digest are agent-authored: an agent turn
composes the board. The roster and the live room transcript are deterministic boards a
collector or the room driver assembles. What they have in common is the contract, not
the author. Each is a board published to a `rib:chamber:*` key and drawn by the canvas.
The briefing was the first proof an agent could author its own view, and it still runs
on demand on its own key, distinct from the per-subject lenses described here.
