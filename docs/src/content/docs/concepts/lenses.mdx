A **lens** is a view a Mind authors itself. One agent turn composes a canvas board
and publishes it, and it renders as a live panel on the Chamber surface with no
hand-coded UI. The view is produced by an agent, not written by hand. It is the
distinctive last move of a Chamber session: the reasoning does not just leave a
transcript, it leaves a live board you can keep open.

## A lens is the output of a turn

A lens is not a participant and not an agent. It is the product of a single Mind's
turn, the same way genesis produces a Mind and a room turn produces a reply. Because
it is a solo turn with no peer, a lens is orthogonal to rooms: it does not ride the
room driver and does not depend on a strategy. (Minds talking to each other is a
[room](../rooms/); a Mind authoring a view is a turn.)

## Lens, transcript, and briefing

A lens is one of several boards on the Chamber surface. It helps to know how it
differs from the others by author and update model:

| Artifact | Producer | Purpose | Update model |
|---|---|---|---|
| Room board | The room driver | Show the attributed conversation | Updated after each committed turn |
| Lens | A Mind or the lens workflow | Preserve an interpretation or a decision | Re-authored explicitly under a stable id |
| Briefing | The rib's attention gate plus one agent turn | Surface newly ended rooms or changed lenses | Runs only when its watermark finds new substance |
| Roster and index boards | Deterministic collectors | Show persistent Chamber state | Cadence or explicit refresh |

## The board, and the pipeline it rides

A board is a generic canvas payload: a title, an optional status, and a few kinds
of section (stats, rows, cards, segments, and so on). It is the harness's own
shape, not Chamber's, and the harness already knows how to render one. See the
keelson docs for the board contract.

Authoring a lens uses the same pipeline as everything else in Chamber. The turn
emits a board, the rib validates it fail-closed before anything renders, and the
bound view draws it live. A board that does not validate is rejected loudly rather
than rendered half-formed or dropped without a word.

:::caution[Validation protects the canvas contract, not the conclusion]
A valid lens has the correct board shape. Its claims are still agent-authored
judgments. Weigh them against the room transcript and the lens's provenance, not the
fact that it rendered cleanly.
:::

## A panel per subject

Each lens is keyed by a short subject id, and each new subject gets its own panel
on the Chamber surface. Re-authoring the same subject updates that panel in place
rather than adding another. A lens is a persistent published view, not a background
monitor: it changes only when a Mind or workflow authors the same id again, never on
its own. The rib sets no fixed limit on how many lenses can exist; the harness
enforces a per-surface ceiling.

## Provenance the card carries

A lens can record who authored it and why, so the Lenses index reads as a living
library rather than an anonymous pile. The authoring turn may attach three optional
fields: a **scope** (the board's kind, like "status board" or "checklist"), a
**reason** (a short note on what the latest authoring changed), and, when a Mind
authors a lens mid-room, a **maintainingMind** (its own name). The index card shows
each one when present, alongside an "updated" time the server stamps on every write.
None of it is required, and re-authoring a subject without a field clears whatever
was there before.

## Two ways to author one

- **As a workflow.** Run `/lens <subject>`, or
  `keelson workflow run chamber-lens "<subject>"`. One turn composes a board for
  the subject and publishes it. This is the standalone entry point.
- **During a room.** A Mind that declares the `lens` capability can author a lens
  mid-room, for example to surface a findings summary after a discussion. A Mind
  with no declared capability is text-only, so this is opt-in per Mind. See
  [Minds and genesis](../minds/) for declaring capabilities.

## Every panel uses the same canvas contract

Every panel on the Chamber surface is a published board, never hand-coded UI, but they
do not all share an author. A lens and the briefing are agent-authored: an agent turn
composes the board. The roster and the live room transcript are deterministic boards a
collector or the room driver assembles. What they have in common is the contract, not
the author. Each is a board published to a `rib:chamber:*` key and drawn by the canvas.
The briefing was the first proof an agent could author its own view, and it still runs
on demand on its own key, distinct from the per-subject lenses described here.
