This is the mental model behind Chamber. It assumes you know the
[Keelson](https://danielscholl.github.io/keelson/) harness and spends its words on
what Chamber adds: where a room fits, the path from a question to a durable outcome,
and the four ideas underneath it.

## Where a room fits

Keelson already runs agents two ways. Chamber adds a third, for the work the other
two cannot check on their own.

| Mode | Use it for | Control flow |
|---|---|---|
| **Chat** | Fast, open-ended work with one agent | The agent decides |
| **Workflow** | Repeatable execution with known steps | The YAML decides |
| **Chamber room** | Decisions worth challenging from several perspectives | A room strategy decides |

Rooms are emergent and non-deterministic, which is the point. They are the
generative counterpart to workflows, not a replacement: repeatable operations still
belong in workflows, and a room is where you want exploration, debate, or a second
opinion. The two compose, and Keelson already owns the deterministic half. See
[when to convene a room](when-to-convene-a-room/) for the call itself.

## From question to durable outcome

Chamber's pieces are not three adjacent features. They are stages of one path, from
a plain-language brief to something you can act on and keep.

<Steps>

1. **Brief.** You describe a perspective you want, in plain language.
2. **Minds.** Genesis authors that brief into a persistent specialist agent. You
   author a Mind once and reuse it across many rooms.
3. **Room and strategy.** You convene several Minds, and a strategy routes their
   turns.
4. **Attributed transcript.** The driver records who argued what, turn by turn.
5. **Lens or briefing.** A Mind can publish the result as a live board on the
   canvas, so it outlives the transcript.
6. **Outcome.** You act on it: a human decision, or a deterministic workflow that
   carries it out.

</Steps>

Read the stages by what each one preserves. A **Mind** preserves a perspective. A
**room** tests perspectives against one another. A **lens** preserves the useful
result outside the transcript. All of it persists as inspectable files: the Minds,
the saved transcript, and any lenses they author.

## The model

Four ideas carry the rest of the rib.

- **Minds** are durable points of view: persistent specialist agents you author from
  a brief, each with a stable identity and an optional pinned model.
- **Rooms** are governed exchanges between those points of view: you convene Minds
  and they take turns on one topic.
- **Strategies** encode the shape of the exchange: sequential, concurrent,
  group-chat, open-floor, or a cross-vendor review.
- **Lenses** turn a conclusion into a visible artifact: a Mind authors a live board
  on the canvas, so the judgment does not stay trapped in the transcript.

## Boundaries and tradeoffs

Every room turn is a paid agent call, so a room earns its cost or it does not run.
The basic call:

Reach for Chamber when:

- A plan carries assumptions that should be challenged before you build on them.
- Distinct roles should defend competing concerns.
- An independent model or provider reading is worth having.
- You want an attributed record of how a decision changed.
- The conclusion should stay visible as a lens.

Prefer something else when:

- One quick answer is enough: use **Chat**.
- The steps are known and should repeat exactly: use a **Workflow**.
- You want an always-running service: a Mind is an identity on disk, not a daemon.
- You need guaranteed correctness: more agents buy more scrutiny, not certainty.
- The extra turns are not worth their cost or latency.

[When to convene a room](when-to-convene-a-room/) goes deeper, including the turn
budget and the confirm-gated dry run that bound a room's spend.

## One pipeline underneath

Everything Chamber renders shares a single path. An agent turn (a room turn or a
lens turn) emits a generic canvas **board**, the rib publishes that board
fail-closed to a snapshot key under `rib:chamber:*`, and the bound view renders it
live. A board is data, not view code, and the canvas already knows how to draw one,
so a Mind can stand up a brand-new panel just by emitting a board.

The invariant is one line: Chamber publishes data through Keelson's canvas contract
rather than shipping its own UI. That is what makes a lens trustworthy and cheap to
extend, not what makes Chamber worth running. For the rest, follow the boundary:
[Design](../design/agent-authored-lenses/) for why it exists, the
[Reference](../reference/surface/) for the exact keys and schemas, and
[Lenses](lenses/) for how agent-authored views use it.
