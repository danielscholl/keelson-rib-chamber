Chamber is a [Keelson](https://danielscholl.github.io/keelson/) **rib**: a
package the harness discovers at runtime and attaches through one typed
contract. It adds a multi-agent operating layer on top of the harness, and it
ships no hand-coded UI. For what a rib is and how the harness loads one, see the
keelson docs; this site assumes that and spends its words on what Chamber adds.

## The mode Chamber adds

Keelson runs agents in two modes. **Chat** is one agent, conversational.
**Workflows** are a deterministic graph of agent steps, repeatable and authored
in YAML. Chamber adds the mode between them: many persistent agents you author
on demand, that talk to each other under a chosen turn strategy, and that render
their own views of what they are doing.

Rooms are emergent and non-deterministic, which is the point. They are the
generative counterpart to workflows, not a replacement. Repeatable operations
still belong in workflows; a room is where you want exploration, debate, or a
second opinion. The two are complementary modes, and keelson already owns the
deterministic half.

## One pipeline underneath

Everything Chamber renders shares a single path. An agent turn (a room turn or a
lens turn) emits a generic canvas **board**, the rib publishes that board
fail-closed to a snapshot key under `rib:chamber:*`, and the bound view renders
it live in the SPA. There is no per-view React: a board is data, and the
harness already knows how to draw one. The board shape and the canvas renderer
are keelson's; Chamber only produces the payloads.

That single pipeline is what makes the hero capability honest. When an agent
authors a lens, it emits the same kind of board the room transcript and the
briefing do, so its view renders through machinery that already exists.

## Zero hand-coded UI

The roster, the live room transcript, the briefing, and every agent-authored
lens are boards published to `rib:chamber:*` keys and drawn by the canvas
renderer. None of them is React shipped from the rib. This is the invariant the
whole rib protects: the surface is produced by running agents and the harness
canvas, not by view code.
