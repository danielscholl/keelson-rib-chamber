One agent can produce a clear answer and still carry one set of assumptions all
the way through. A deterministic workflow can make that answer repeatable, but it
cannot challenge the premise it was handed.

Chamber adds structured multi-agent review to Keelson. You author persistent
specialist agents, convene them around a plan or a decision, and choose how they
take turns, challenge each other, or review work across providers. Chamber keeps
the attributed transcript, and a Mind can turn the result into a live lens on the
canvas.

Use Chamber to red-team a design, review a contract before implementation, compare
competing approaches, or force a plan to survive a defender, a skeptic, and an
operator before a workflow acts on it.

**Minds** are persistent perspectives. **Rooms** put those perspectives into a
governed conversation. **Lenses** keep the resulting judgment visible.

Chamber is a [Keelson](https://danielscholl.github.io/keelson/) rib: it installs
into the harness you already run and reuses what the harness owns, its providers,
the workflow engine, and the canvas, so a Mind renders a live view with no
hand-coded UI. Everything here is what Chamber adds.
