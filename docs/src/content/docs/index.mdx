Chamber gives Keelson a multi-agent operating layer. You author persistent
agents (**Minds**) on demand from a plain-language brief, put them in a **room**
to take turns on a problem under a strategy you choose, and let them author their
own **lenses**: live views they draw onto the canvas as they work.

It is a [Keelson](https://danielscholl.github.io/keelson/) rib, so it installs
into the harness you already run and reuses what the harness owns (its providers,
the workflow engine, and the canvas). Everything here is what Chamber adds.

:::tip[New here?]
Start with [Concepts](concepts/) for the model, or jump straight to
[your first room](tutorials/your-first-room/) to build something. The
[Guides](guides/) are task recipes, and the [Reference](reference/) is the exact
contract.
:::
